Onsite Intelligence

Four models. One system. Fundable outcomes.

Humanitai combines four proven onsite intelligence models into a single pipeline: community health workers collect ground truth, MiroFish predicts where crisis will hit, Social Impact Bonds fund the intervention, and SROI proves the return. Every pound tracked. Every outcome verified.

Model 1: Community Health Worker Intelligence

Proven globally — 1.3M+ CHWs worldwide (WHO). Brazil's Family Health Program cut infant mortality 42%. Iran's CHW network cut maternal mortality by 73%. We deploy trained community liaisons into UK neighbourhoods to collect real-time ground truth: needs assessments, intake forms, photo evidence, location-stamped reports. Not estimates. Verified data from the street.

Model 2: MiroFish Swarm Prediction

Onsite data feeds into our multi-agent swarm intelligence model. Seven dimensions analysed — health, housing, food, employment, digital exclusion, education, isolation. Five agent clusters debate from different perspectives to find where interventions will prevent crisis. Confidence-scored forecasts. This is the same pattern RAND Corporation identified: predict where need will spike, deploy resources before it does.

Model 3: Social Impact Bond Financing

UK pioneered SIBs in 2010 at Peterborough Prison. 276 SIBs now operate in 23 countries, $745M capital deployed. Investors fund interventions upfront. Government pays back only if outcomes are achieved. Humanitai is the delivery and measurement layer — the ground truth data that proves outcomes. No results, no payment. Funders take zero risk on promises.

Model 4: SROI Outcome Measurement

Social Return on Investment — the UK-government-recognised framework, 8 principles from Social Value UK. We convert social outcomes into monetary value so funders can compare like for like. "For every £1 invested, £X social value returned." This is what makes Humanitai fundable: not outputs ("200 people served") but outcomes ("85% sustained improvement at 6 months, £4.20 social value per £1 invested").

MiroFish Forecast + SROI

Where the money goes. Where it should go. What it returns.

Primary Target: Mental Health Gap, Ages 16-29

Our swarm intelligence model analysed UK social data across seven dimensions — ONS health data, NHS waiting lists, JRF poverty figures, demographic trajectories. It found one clear target: the mental health support gap for young adults in underserved communities.

Anxiety and depression rates have doubled in a decade. With 20% of children already affected and NHS services at capacity, early intervention and community-based support could prevent a significant portion of crisis cases before they escalate into the system's most expensive failures.

Using SROI methodology (Social Return on Investment — the UK-standard framework with 8 principles from Social Value UK), every pound invested in early mental health intervention returns an estimated multiple in social value: reduced emergency care demand, improved productivity, prevented crisis escalation. The model forecasts this approach could save the system far more than it costs.
