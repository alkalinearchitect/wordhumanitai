The architecture

Four layers. One pipeline.

OSINT data gathering → multi-agent swarm prediction → community health ground truth → SROI-verified outcomes.

Layer 01

OSINT Data

More than a dozen public datasets from ONS, NHS England, the Trussell Trust, the Joseph Rowntree Foundation and gov.uk — gathered by API, browser automation and open-source intelligence.

Layer 02

Human Architect

A multi-agent swarm with a Neo4j knowledge graph and social simulation, scoring forecasts across seven dimensions with explicit confidence intervals — not a single black box.

Layer 03

Community Health Teams

Trained workers in high-risk neighbourhoods verify predictions with real needs assessments, photo evidence and location-stamped reports. The machine proposes; people confirm.

Layer 04

SIB + SROI

Social Impact Bonds fund the intervention. The UK-government-recognised SROI framework verifies every pound. Investors are paid only when outcomes are achieved.

Straight answers

What people ask before they back us.

What exactly does Humanitai do?
We deploy trained community health intelligence teams into UK neighbourhoods with the worst verified outcomes. They collect real ground-truth data — needs assessments, intake forms, photo evidence — which feeds our Human Architect technology (OSINT plus multi-agent AI) to predict where crisis will hit next.
Why not just give the data to the council?
Because the data already exists and still nothing changes. Humanitai adds the measurement and outcome layer that Social Impact Bonds require. Funders pay only when verified outcomes are achieved.
Is this another charity asking for donations?
No. We are a Community Interest Company — asset-locked, dividend-capped. Built to be funded through Social Impact Bonds: investors fund interventions, government repays only on proven outcomes.
How do you prove the outcomes are real?
SROI — the UK-government-recognised eight-principle framework from Social Value UK. Every pound tracked. Every outcome verified.
Where do you operate?
Currently mapping eighteen UK regions — from Glasgow to Newham, Bristol to Blackpool. See the intelligence dashboard above.
