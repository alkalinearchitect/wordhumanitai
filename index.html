Where It Breaks

Six crises. One broken system.

The UK spends £1.2 trillion a year. Yet millions fall through gaps that data and AI could close. Here's where the system fails.

1 in 6 adults · 20% of children

Mental Health Collapse

1 in 6 adults experience a common mental health disorder weekly. 20% of children aged 8-16 have a probable disorder — up from 12% in 2017. The system is drowning.

354,000 homeless · +7.6% YoY

Homelessness Rising

132,410 households in temporary accommodation — a record. Shelter counts 354,000 people homeless in England, including 161,500 children. Numbers climbing every quarter.

2.89M food parcels · 7.5M food insecure

Food Insecurity

Trussell Trust supplied 2.89 million emergency food parcels in 2024/25. 13.9% of UK households were food insecure in January 2025. Parents skip meals so children can eat.

88% cite cost of living · 5M in negative budget

Cost of Living Crisis

88% of UK adults say cost of living is a top issue. Prices rose 20.8% between 2021-2024. Nearly 5 million people spend more than they earn. 2.35 million are "living on empty."

1.01M NEET · 13.5% of 16-24s

Youth Disconnection

Over 1 million 16-24 year olds are NEET — first time above 1M since 2013. 61% are economically inactive, the highest on record. An entire generation losing momentum.

£19.9B housing vs £360.9B welfare

Systemic Under-Investment

The UK spends £291 per person on housing but £5,284 on welfare. Under-investment at the front end creates costly crises at the back end. The math doesn't work.

MiroFish Prediction Model

AI-Powered Impact Forecast

Our swarm intelligence model analyzed UK social data across seven dimensions to predict where Humanitai can create maximum impact. Here is what it found.

Primary Target: Mental Health Support Gap (Ages 16-29)

Based on analysis of ONS health data, NHS waiting lists, JRF poverty figures, and demographic trajectories, our model identifies a critical opportunity: bridging the mental health support gap for young adults in underserved communities.

Anxiety and depression rates have doubled in a decade. With 20% of children already affected and NHS mental health services at capacity, AI-powered early intervention and community-based support networks could prevent a significant portion of crisis cases before they escalate.

The model predicts this approach could reduce emergency care demand, improve productivity, and address root causes before they become the annual burden the UK currently carries.
